# .impeccable.md

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary:** Moj Mahdara - CEO of Kinship Ventures, serial founder, relationship-driven operator. Opens the app like checking Oura in the morning - a daily health check on her network. She manages ~150 meaningful relationships across investors, talent, service providers, creatives, and family.

**Secondary:** Moj's team (Briell, others) - operations people who need to understand the network, log interactions, and manage data entry. They see the same tool but through a contributor lens.

**Future:** Moj wants to share this with friends - other founders and operators who manage relationships the same way. The tool should feel personal enough that someone would want their own.

**Context:** The user opens this when they have 5 minutes between meetings, or as a morning ritual. They're checking: who needs attention, what's the health of my network, who should I reach out to today. The job is maintaining and deepening existing relationships - not acquiring new ones.

**Emotional goal:** Energized and in control. Like checking a fitness tracker that shows you're doing well - or gives you a clear, motivating nudge when you're not. Never guilt. Never overwhelm. Always agency.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Warm. Bold. Human.

**Voice:** The app speaks like a thoughtful friend, not a task manager. "You haven't talked to Sarah in 42 days. That's not like you." - not "Sarah Chen - last contacted: 42 days ago."

**Emotional register:**
- Celebratory when things are going well (Spotify Wrapped energy)
- Gentle but honest when things need attention (Oura readiness score)
- Never clinical, never nagging, never guilt-tripping
- Personality in the data - numbers should feel alive, not sterile

**From Moj:** "Is it too soon to be like, make everything bolder?"

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Expressive, alive, confident. Vibrant colors are earned - they appear on things that represent health, activity, and attention. They're signal, not decoration.

**Design references:**
| Reference | What to take from it |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Oura Ring | Health scoring dashboard feel - readiness, streaks, debt. Personal data presented beautifully. Calm authority. |
| Spotify Wrapped | Visual energy - personal, colorful, celebratory. Your data as a story, not a spreadsheet. |
| Trolley CRM | The actual design deck Moj handed over - serif authority, editorial weight, bold color. |
| Montessori | Self-explanatory UI, intuitive, no manual needed. Everything teaches itself through interaction. |

**Anti-references:**
| Reference | Why it's rejected |
|-----------|------------------|
| HubSpot | "Sterile, corporate, just wants you to email people." |
| Generic CRM | "It looks just like HubSpot" is a death sentence. |
| Spreadsheet UI | Contacts shouldn't feel like rows in a table. |
| Original Lovable build | "Very corporate. Does not feel as fun. No one's going to want to engage with it." |

**Theme:** Light mode primary (warm off-white #F5F4F0), dark mode supported via `prefers-color-scheme`. Not a toggle - respects system preference.

**Typography:** Fraunces (serif) for headings - editorial authority, weight, intention. Plus Jakarta Sans for body - geometric, tall x-height, contemporary. The serif/sans pairing gives it personality that no CRM has.

**Color:** Brand green (#25B439) is confident and present - used for identity and signal, not decoration. Pod colors are expressive and varied. Interaction types have distinct semantic colors.

**Texture:** Subtle grain overlay on body (SVG noise at 2.8% opacity). Adds physicality - the interface feels like a surface, not a screen.

### Design Principles

1. **Relationships are people, not records.** Every design decision should make contacts feel like humans you care about, not rows in a database. Names in serif. Avatars with personality. Context over metadata.

2. **Health is visible at a glance.** Equity scores, health rings, color-coded states - the network's health should be readable in 2 seconds. Progressive disclosure: glance first, drill into detail on demand.

3. **Boldness is earned.** Vibrant colors, large type, and visual weight appear where they signal something meaningful - health, attention needed, celebration. Quiet where nothing needs saying.

4. **Feel like a morning ritual, not a chore.** Opening the app should trigger the same feeling as checking your Oura score - curiosity, motivation, agency. Never guilt, never overwhelm.

5. **Montessori, not manual.** Every interaction should teach itself. If you need a tooltip to explain it, the design needs work. Drag, click, explore - the UI reveals itself through use.

### Existing Design System

The project has a mature token system documented in `docs/design-system.md` and implemented in `src/index.css`. Key elements:

- **Tokens:** CSS custom properties for all colors, shadows, spacing, motion
- **Dark mode:** Full adaptive alpha token system (black/white flip)
- **Motion:** Four easing curves (enter, decel, panel, press) with stagger delays
- **Components:** Orbs (solid gradient spheres), pod cards, contact panels, dashboard sections
- **Accessibility:** WCAG AA target, focus rings, reduced motion support, contrast-validated tokens
- **Interaction classes:** Behavioral taxonomy (action-ghost, action-pill, interactive-row, close-trigger)
- **8pt grid:** Spacing in multiples of 8

### What "Good" Looks Like

- A dashboard that makes you want to reach out to someone
- Pod cards that feel like they represent real groups of people, not folders
- Orbs that feel alive - like they're breathing with the health of the relationships inside them
- Scores that motivate, not judge
- Empty states that inspire action, not feel lonely
- Transitions that feel confident and intentional, never flashy

### What "Bad" Looks Like

- Anything that could be mistaken for Salesforce, HubSpot, or a generic SaaS dashboard
- Gray, corporate, sterile interfaces
- Data presented without personality or context
- Modals and forms that feel like enterprise software
- Generic placeholder copy ("No results found", "Loading...")
