## What This Page Needs To Do

The dashboard should help the user decide what matters today.

That means:
- one strong opening point
- one clear action area
- everything else in support of that

## What The Current Dashboard Already Gets Right

- the page is useful
- there is a lot of operational value on one screen
- the current top section already points toward a calmer product feel
- the user can scan pressure, movement, replies, and waiting from one place

## What The Dashboard Is Missing

- one stronger thesis at the top
- a clearer sense of what is primary and what is secondary
- more composure across sections
- less feeling of stacked modules

## Current Anatomy

From the live current design:

### Top Utility Strip

- date and timezone
- quick source status
- small home/control toggle

### Hero / Thesis Area

- large statement about the day
- compact summary counts
- source cards on the right

### Main Body

- "what deserves attention first?"
- "which world are you stepping into?"
- replies
- radar
- waiting on
- recent movement
- loose ends

## What To Keep

- the strong headline at the top
- the split between urgent work and world-based context
- the calmer white surfaces
- the idea that this page is about pressure, movement, and drift

## What To Change

### Top Of Page

- make the top strip feel more integrated with the shell
- tighten the relationship between the utility strip and the thesis area
- reduce the feeling that they are two separate boards

### Hero Area

- make the thesis feel even more like the page anchor
- keep the right-side signals compact
- make sure the eye lands in one place first

### Mid-Page Hierarchy

- the attention area should clearly outrank the rest
- the worlds column should feel like a supporting rail, not a competing page
- replies, radar, waiting, and movement need a cleaner order

### Overall Rhythm

- use more deliberate spacing between section groups
- reduce the feeling of endless stacked cards
- make the page feel composed, not just full

## Target Direction

Dia calm for the thesis area.

Arc shell for the page frame.

Real Deal utility for the actual content.

## Dashboard Rules

- one main thought first
- one main action cluster second
- supporting modules should step down cleanly
- right-column support areas should stay quieter
- green can frame the page, but the reading surfaces should stay neutral

## First Redesign Moves

1. Merge the top utility strip and thesis area into one cleaner opening frame.
2. Make the attention section the clear primary work area.
3. Push supporting context into a more disciplined side structure.
4. Rebalance the lower sections so the page stops feeling long too early.
