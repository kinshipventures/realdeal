# RealDeal - Vision & Philosophy

Source: Synthesized from 9 Granola meeting transcripts (Feb 17 - Mar 30, 2026), in-session brainstorming, and project docs.

---

## The Core Belief

"The most important thing on the planet is your social relationship. You are going to literally make or break your life based on how you treat your relationships. Starting now. Not later, now."

This app exists to feed what feeds you.

---

## What This Is

A relationship-first operating system for founders and operators. Not a CRM - a living network map that reflects how you actually think about your relationships.

One place where every relationship lives with full context - so you never lose track of who matters, what happened, or what's next.

The relationship record is the only core object. Pipelines, campaigns, follow-ups, and projects attach to relationships, never replace them. The system behaves like a trusted relationship manager, not a sales tool.

## What This Is NOT

- Not a CRM. Not HubSpot. Not a sales pipeline.
- Not a tool for having more friends. It's for having fewer, better ones.
- Not a networking bro tool. "This is the opposite of Never Eat Alone."
- Not a spreadsheet with a UI on top.
- Not about scraped data or enrichment APIs. It's human-inputted context.

---

## The Name

Three tiers of people in your life:
- **Real friends** - unconditional. Would stay if you worked at Trader Joe's.
- **Deal friends** - transactional. The relationship exists because of business.
- **Real Deal friends** - genuine AND business. The overlap.

The app helps you see which is which, and invest accordingly.

---

## Five Guiding Principles

### 1. Give more than you take
The app is about contributing to relationships, not extracting from them. Build equity so when you need something, you've already invested. "90% of the time I'm going to talk to you is not for me to sell you something. It's for you to sell me something, so that when the one time I need something from you, you give it to me."

### 2. Trust is built on small micro habits, not grand gestures
Consistent check-ins, a quick congrats, reposting someone's content - these compound over time. The app should make those habits effortless and visible.

### 3. Trigger dopamine
The best relationships happen when people associate you with positivity, good news, shared passions, and excitement. The app should help you be that person.

### 4. Curate ruthlessly
A smaller, healthier network beats a large neglected one. Max 20 per list. If someone hasn't been contacted in 3+ months and neither side is investing, they should be removed. "This isn't about how big this list is. This is about how small this is."

### 5. Relationship debt is real
Like Oura's sleep debt - if you're not investing, you're withdrawing. The app makes that visible. You can quantify it. You can fix it.

## Why This Matters (Moj's Story)

"My ability to regenerate like a fucking gremlin over and over again is because other people believe in me."

Moj's companies crashed multiple times. Relationships are literally what let her rebuild each time. This isn't abstract philosophy - it's survival strategy from lived experience.

"I could call anyone on this list and be like, I need you to wire me ten grand. I can't tell you why, but I'm in a jam." That's the level of trust she's built through consistent micro-investment.

She lost $150K in management fees in one year due to relationship management inefficiencies - should have made $300K, only made $150K on a $5M raise. The business cost is real and quantified.

"Everyone I need is one person away. I don't need to meet anyone new." The tool deepens existing relationships, not expands the network.

## Core Metaphors

### Social Equity (like a bank account)
Your relationships have a balance. Every interaction is a deposit or withdrawal. Are you in the black or the red? The app is your relationship bank statement.

### Relationship Debt (like Oura sleep debt)
If you're not investing, you're withdrawing. The dashboard should feel like checking your Oura readiness score - but for relationships.

### Trust Transfer (the chain)
Trust moves from person to person through the network. One person vouches for you, and that trust transfers to the next relationship. This chain of trust is how networks actually function.

### The Human Guide
"This is, in a way, like a human guide. It's a way to rate your friends and your relationships. But you're also grading yourself."

## How Moj Actually Uses Relationships

### Reaching out during hardship
"90% of my relationships are people that I got closer with when they were in a shitty place. Anytime someone gets canceled, fired, terminated, goes bankrupt, goes through divorce... I always send them a note. Always."

This is a core behavior the tool should support - not just birthday reminders and follow-ups, but knowing when someone needs support.

### The 90/10 rule
90% of interactions are about giving - sharing opportunities, making intros, sending congratulations. 10% is asking for something. By the time you ask, you've already invested so much that the answer is always yes.

### Intros as the highest form of giving
Connecting two people is the most valuable thing you can do. The interaction hierarchy reflects this: intros carry the most weight in equity scoring.

---

## Interaction Hierarchy

Not all interactions are equal. Ranked by relationship weight:

1. **Intro** (weight: 5) - connecting two people is the highest form of giving
2. **Meeting** (weight: 4) - face-to-face time
3. **Call** (weight: 3) - real-time voice connection
4. **Text / Email** (weight: 2) - digital check-in
5. **Note** (weight: 0) - internal only, doesn't count toward equity

## Design Philosophy

### Two moods, one soul
- **Map = the quiet room** - where you reflect on network structure
- **Dashboard = the morning briefing** - where the network talks back with energy and personality

### Aesthetic direction
- Spotify Wrapped energy - vibrant gradients, bold, expressive, celebratory
- NOT corporate, NOT sterile, NOT "just like HubSpot"
- Alive and human, not clinical
- Vibrant colors are earned - they appear on things that represent health, activity, attention needed. They're signal, not decoration.
- Montessori-inspired interaction - self-explanatory, drag-and-drop, no instruction manual needed

### Design taste references
| Reference | What it means |
|-----------|--------------|
| Oura Ring | Health scoring dashboard feel - readiness, streaks, debt |
| Spotify Wrapped | Visual energy - personal, colorful, celebratory |
| Trolley CRM | The actual design deck handed over as "make it look like this" |
| Montessori | Self-explanatory UI, intuitive, no manual needed |

### Anti-references
| Reference | Why it's rejected |
|-----------|------------------|
| HubSpot | "Sterile, corporate, just wants you to email people" |
| Generic CRM | "It looks just like HubSpot" is a death sentence |
| Spreadsheet UI | Contacts shouldn't feel like rows in a table |
| The original Lovable build | "Very corporate. Does not feel as fun. No one's going to want to engage with it." |

## The App's Voice

Personality in the interface. Not clinical, not robotic.

- Good: "You haven't talked to Sarah in 42 days. That's not like you."
- Bad: "Sarah Chen - last contacted: 42 days ago."

The app should feel like a thoughtful friend reminding you to reach out, not a task manager nagging you about overdue items.

---

## Success Criteria

1. **Moj uses it daily** - it becomes a morning habit, like checking email
2. **Team gets it** - Briell can look at it and immediately understand the network and what needs attention
3. **Behavior changes** - check-ins happen more consistently. Relationships actually get healthier because of this tool.
